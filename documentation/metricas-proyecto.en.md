## Populations (do not mix denominators)

The six denominators respond to different questions and are not interchangeable:

- 216 evaluable (Stage accuracy) = the 247 delayed minus the 31 Indeterminate, which do not have
  a measurable dominant gap.
- 203 classifiable (Reason agreement) = delayed with non-null human annotation `reason_group_manual`; nulls are mapped to "Unknown" and excluded from the denominator.
- 20 sampled (LLM Explanation Quality) = stratified sample 8/4/4/4
  (Vendor/Carrier/Indeterminate/DC), seed 42, reproducible.
- 14 hot-late (Severity Ranking) = POs with `HOT_PO_FLAG=1` and `delay_days_calc > 3`.
- 247 delayed with tier-2 populated (Differentiation) = subset of the 247 delayed with
  `llm_hipotesis`/`llm_accion_inmediata` computed (requires an `--action-call` run).
- 247 delayed (Distribution) = complete population of delayed POs.

The Indeterminate in the distribution (31) is broken down into 7 `sin_datos` (without trailer time) + 24
`sin_causa_dominante` (measurable but not exceeding any threshold). *(ADR-03b closing note,
2026-07-22: the ADR↔repo audit found a `decidible` gate that excluded vendor without its own
condition; the fix moved 8 POs from `sin_datos` to Vendor. See
[ADR-03b](decisiones/ARD-03b.en.md).)*

## Notes

- The official severity of the deliverable is that of the LLM (`severity ← llm_severidad`, ADR-10); the
  deterministic rule of F2 is preserved as an audit baseline in the internal artifact
  and gives 14/14 by construction (measures ranking, not match with the LLM).
- Reason agreement has no mentor threshold: it is the thesis of the project. The disagreement with the
  human annotation (~20% incorrect, data from the kickoff) is expected and desired; the 23 mismatches
  are evidence that the temporal computation by timestamps exceeds manual annotation.
- LLM Explanation Quality: the headline figure (5/5, 20/20) corresponds to the production setting — few-shot C3 at temperature 0.9, which generates `po_output.csv` — with human validation. It is the endpoint of a progression measured against the same benchmark (seed 42):
  zero-shot 3.25/5 (13/20) → few-shot C3 at temp 0.3 (benchmark for selecting the winning combination)
  4.75/5 (19/20) → tightening the prompt in #143 closes the only failure
  (PO 100182, stage Indeterminate) to 5/5 (20/20), even at temp 0.3 → ADR-13 re-validates that figure
  at the actual production temperature (0.9) without regression. The 4.75/5 is not discarded: it is the
  milestone that demonstrated that C3 surpasses the mentor's goal (4/5) and won against C1/C2; the 5/5 is the
  figure that describes what the deliverable produces today.
- Severity: divergence LLM vs rule F2. Among the 247 delayed, `severity` (LLM, the official one)
  coincides with F2's deterministic severity in 213/247 (86.2%). The 34 divergences
  (13.8%) **always escalate** — they never de-escalate: 30 cases LOW→MEDIUM, 4 cases MEDIUM→HIGH.
  Reading: the LLM exercises additional judgment on aggravating factors that the fixed rule does not capture
  (e.g., combinations of hot PO / short ship / borderline delay), and that judgment never lowers
  the alert compared to the rule — it is a finding, not a defect. Reproduction without spending API:
  compare the `severity` column of `po_output.csv` (or `llm_severidad` in
  `df_with_llm_full_openai.csv`) against the `severity` of `df_classified.csv` (F2), by
  `PO_NBR`. Narrative detail in `documentation/validacion-y-qa.md`.
- Intra-stage differentiation (#151): finding, not a mentor threshold. Among the 247 delayed
  with tier-2 populated, 65 (26.3%) receive their stage's modal hypothesis even though their
  evidence signature (short-ship, hot PO, match with REASON_DSC) differs from that mode, and
  51 (20.6%) the same for immediate action. This is ignored evidence, not a computation error:
  stage and severity remain correct. Detail in
  `03_llm_integration/eval_differentiation.md`.
