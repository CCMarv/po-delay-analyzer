# Conditional Domain Context by (actor × signal) to Diversify Phase 3 Prompt

* **Status:** 📘 **Superseded** by [ADR-16](ARD-16.en.md) (2026-07-06)
* **Technical Context:** Phase 3 / LLM Integration — enrichment of the prompt so that `recommended_action` varies among POs of the same responsible party
* **References:** Issue #151 (matured from #143/#154); #94 (quality benchmark, 20 POs); ADR-12 (few-shot prompt design); ADR-13 (temperature — sampling ruled out as a cause/fix); ADR-14 (anti-overfitting hardening of the prompt, whose closure of HOW TO REASON makes this decision conditional); ADR-07 (taxonomy of Indeterminate, reused sub-stages as routing signals); ADR-04b/ADR-06b (carrier/vendor thresholds, single source in `rules_config.json`); `03_llm_integration/llm_integration.py` (`build_prompt`, `select_domain_context`, `_cond_matches`, `_excess_band`); `03_llm_integration/domain_kb.json`; `03_llm_integration/eval_diversity.py`

## Context and Problem

The `recommended_action` generated by the LLM for delayed POs is accurate and operable, but homogeneous within each stage: it barely differentiates between POs of the same responsible party (#143, matured in #151/#154). ADR-13 ruled out sampling (temperature) as the cause: the sweep of 0.3–0.9 did not diversify enough on its own. The prompt already includes guidance by actor (`HOW TO REASON`, ADR-14), yet the model converges to a canonical phrasing per stage — indicating a lack of context that distinguishes one case from another within the same stage; general domain knowledge is already covered by that guidance.

A key finding informed the design: more shared knowledge per actor does not diversify (it is identical for all POs of that actor; if anything, it homogenizes further). Diversity can only come from what already differs among POs — the signals per-PO that Phase 2 emits (excess magnitude band, `is_short_ship`, `is_rescheduled`, `HOT_PO_FLAG`, sub-stage of DC/Indeterminate).

## Considered Options (enrichment mechanism)

**Option A — More shared domain context per actor.** Pros: simple. Cons: identical for all POs of the actor → does not diversify; based on previous diagnosis, likely to homogenize further.

**Option B — RAG / semantic retrieval over a knowledge base.** Pros: scales to a large and unstructured database. Cons: the routing key (actor + per-PO signal) is already computed deterministically by Phase 2, there is no semantic retrieval problem to resolve; adds a new dependency (embeddings/vector store) unnecessarily; synthetic entities (fictional vendors/carriers) cause a web search by entity to return noise, not signal.

**Option C — Versioned JSON + deterministic lookup indexed by (actor × per-PO signal) (chosen).** Pros: the routing key already exists; “retrieve” is a dictionary lookup, not a search; auditable and versioned, consistent with `rules_config.json`/`fewshot_pool.json`. Cons: requires manual content curation; limited coverage to signals already modeled.

## Considered Options (content level within the KB)

**Option A — Finished actions repertoire.** Pros: more predictable output. Cons: reduces the call to the LLM to paraphrasing and only changes the exact words of the output, without enriching the reasoning of cause or action. Rejected after feedback: overly constrains possible responses.

**Option B — Levers + domain tensions synthesized by the LLM (chosen).** Pros: the KB provides input (operational lever, tension to weigh — e.g., a reschedule may be a consequence of a downstream miss, not a cause of the vendor), but the synthesis of cause and action anchored to PO figures is done by the LLM; preserves the analytical role of the call. Cons: more variance in final wording.

## Considered Options (call architecture)

**Option A — 2 calls (differentiate + write).** Pros: separates context selection and drafting. Cons: doubles cost/latency per PO; no evidence that 1 call is insufficient.

**Option B — 1 call; the KB routing already varies the prompt (chosen).** Pros: same cost as current production. Cons: if `eval_diversity` showed it was not sufficient, scaling would be required (2-call architecture pre-designed, not implemented).

## Decision

1. **KB as a constrained repertoire indexed by (actor × signal).** `domain_kb.json` (versioned) provides, by actor (Vendor/Carrier/DC/Indeterminate), a short `first` and a list of `levers`, each with a `cond` on PO signals. `select_domain_context` filters those fulfilled by the PO via `_cond_matches` (deterministic, fail-closed for unknown keys).
2. **Excess magnitude bands.** `_excess_band` derives low/medium/high from `r = excess / Phase2_threshold`, cuts at {1, 3} calibrated against actual distribution. Phase 2 thresholds from `rules_config.json` (single source). Indeterminate has no band (excess removed from its prompt in ADR-14).
3. **Content at "levers + tensions" level, not finished actions.** The injected block explicitly instructs “do not transcribe them”.
4. **1 call per PO.** With `kb=None` (default), the prompt is byte-identical to historical (invariant covered by test). With `kb`, the block `DOMAIN CONTEXT` is added, and the closure of `HOW TO REASON` regarding the action is replaced by a pointer to the levers instead of ADR-14's two fixed illustrative lines (avoids competing as a template; the `kb=None` path remains untouched).
5. **Unit adjustments in the lead-in.** The levers are in hours; validation showed this could prompt omitting the delay in days in `root_cause`. The lead-in (only active with `kb`) now remembers to also cite the days.
6. **Opt-in wiring.** `add_llm_explanations` and `eval_quality.py` (`--kb`) accept `kb` as an optional parameter/flag, default off.

## Validation

Benchmark of 20 stratified POs (seed 42, that of #94/#99), at production temperature (0.9):

| Metric | without kb | with kb |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Automatic pre-evaluation (a & b) | 16/20 | 17/20 |
| Diversity (complete set) | 0.772 | 0.817 |
| Diversity (Vendor subset) | 0.631 | 0.739 |

(c) and the final verdict per PO remain for human validation.

## Consequences

**Positive:** measurable increase in diversity, more marked in Vendor (the subset of the original problem); zero-shot/production behavior does not change except for explicit opt-in; versioned and auditable content; no additional cost/latency over current production.

**Negative:** the KB requires manual curation by (actor × signal); limited coverage to signals already modeled today; validation runs over 20 POs at one temperature, does not replace regeneration of the complete deliverable (~247 POs, follow-up with its own API statement); the interaction `kb` × few-shot (C1–C3) was not tested in this round.

## Relation to Other Decisions

Addresses the symptom documented in **ADR-13** (sampling was ruled out as a fix). Returns the closure of `HOW TO REASON` in **ADR-14** to a conditional state, retaining its content. Reuses the taxonomy of **ADR-07** and the thresholds of **ADR-04b/ADR-06b** via `rules_config.json`. Relies on the prompt infrastructure of **ADR-12**.
