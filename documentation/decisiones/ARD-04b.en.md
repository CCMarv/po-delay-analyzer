# ADR-04b · Definitive Carrier Threshold (8h)

* **Status:** 🟢 **CURRENT** (Replaces [ADR-04a · Provisional Carrier Threshold (4h)](ARD-04a.en.md))
* **Technical Context:** Phase 2 / Business Rules Setup
* **References:** Issue #41, Discussion #53, suggested by the mentor on 2026-06-16

## Context and Problem
Following the drop of the 4-hour threshold due to excessive noise in the data (detailed in [ADR-04a](ARD-04a.en.md)), the model required a fair and justified calibration that aligned with the nature of the dataset (short routes) without losing the ability to capture severe impacts in transportation.

## Considered Options

### Option 1: Increase the threshold to 8 hours supported by a sensitivity table (4/6/8/12h) (Chosen)
* **Pros:** Mitigates noise in the data, reducing the flag activation to 12.8% (51 POs), consistent with actual operations. Supported by analytical backing from a sensitivity matrix for future calibrations.
* **Cons:** Requires modifications to the centralized configuration files and documentation of the study to justify the change to stakeholders.

*(Note: Maintaining the 4 hours was discarded due to the false positive issues described).*

## Final Decision
Option **1** was chosen. Following the mentor's suggestion on 2026-06-16 in discussion [#53](https://github.com/CCMarv/po-delay-analyzer/discussions/53), an **8-hour threshold** for the Carrier is definitively established.

This parameter was decoupled from hard coding and was formally persisted in the centralized configuration file **`rules_config.json`**. The analytical change demonstrated stabilization of logistical attribution.

## Consequences
* **Positive:** Drastic reduction of false positives in the transportation segment and increased business confidence in the alerts generated by the classifier.
* **Negative:** Orders that experience delays of between 4 and 7.9 hours will no longer trigger the Carrier flag, requiring secondary monitoring if the business experiences systemic micro-delays.
